Isolated Police Stops By Tract: New York City

Using colocation analysis, this project tested the police justification that pedestrian stops occur in neighborhoods with higher violent crime. Stops were first classified using the Local Colocation Quotient (LCLQ) to identify those that were spatially and temporally isolated from nearby violent crime incidents. A new field was calculated via Arcade expression, flagging significantly isolated stops, then aggregated to census tracts using the Summarize Within tool to produce a value per tract. Dark blue tracts represent areas where the majority of police stops had no proximate violent crime to justify them. A disparity analysis comparing those tracts to citywide averages revealed that, in areas where stops were least tied to crime, Black residents were stopped at higher rates relative to their share of the local population.

Cholera Death Cases Relative to Water Pumps

A two-part georeferenced study of John Snow's landmark 1854 Soho, London map. The first map plots individual cholera deaths and marks water pump locations across the district. The second applies the Thiessen polygons tool to divide the area into proximity zones for each pump.
